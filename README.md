Tata Passenger Electric Mobility: Network Expansion Optimization

Repository Description
This repository contains a data-driven **Business Intelligence Strategy** designed to optimize the expansion of Tata Power’s EZ Charge network. Focusing on the rapid acceleration of India's passenger EV market, this project introduces a strategic framework to solve infrastructure bottlenecks, eliminate consumer charging anxiety, and maximize capital ROI for infrastructure deployment.

The complete analysis and strategic roadmaps can be referenced in the accompanying project file: *"Tata Electric Vehicle Presentation.pdf"*.

---

# Executive Summary of the Presentation
As the undisputed leader in India's passenger EV ecosystem with over 70% market share, Tata Motors faces a critical operational challenge: vehicle sales are growing exponentially, but public charging infrastructure is experiencing uneven, localized strain. This presentation outlines a comprehensive BI strategy to transition infrastructure planning from a reactive model to a predictive, high-turnover deployment model].

The core framework relies on a custom *Infrastructure Sufficiency Index (ISI)*—which measures the ratio of EVs per public charging station—to pinpoint high-strain regions where demand drastically outpaces current asset capacity. 

# Key Findings & Insights
* The Infrastructure Mismatch: The current national baseline stands at an unsustainable *112.11 EVs per public station. This network strain is further compounded by a hardware architecture disproportionately weighted toward low-capacity, slow AC chargers, which extends vehicle dwell times and limits daily throughput.
* Urban vs. Intercity Disparity: Asset distribution mapping reveals heavy infrastructure clustering around major Tier-1 urban centers, leaving massive regional "charging deserts" along critical intercity highways and transit corridors.
* Absolute Capacity Saturation: Multi-year state-level adoption curves are compounding exponentially rather than growing linearly. Infrastructure scaled for today’s demand will hit total capacity saturation within the next 18 months if current trends continue.

# Strategic Roadmap & Recommendations
The presentation concludes with a dual-phase action plan to future-proof the charging grid and sustain passenger EV market growth:

# Phase 1: Quick-Win Optimization
* Alleviate Immediate Congestion: Freeze slow AC deployment in high-strain zones and divert capital to high-turnover fast DC charging plazas.
* Grid Efficiency: Implement dynamic peak/off-peak pricing tariffs to shift fleet charging to low-utilization hours and upgrade high-traffic urban sites from single-plug to dual-plug dispensing.
* Commercial Growth: Secure guaranteed baseline utilization through immediate network partnerships with corporate EV fleet operators and delivery aggregators.

# Phase 2: Long-Term Grid Transformation
* Highway Corridor Mapping: Shift capital allocation from oversaturated urban pockets to a structured highway grid, deploying fast-charging hubs at fixed 50 km intervals to unlock intercity travel and generate a 35% higher utilization rate.
* Smart Infrastructure: Integrate on-site battery energy storage systems (BESS) and captive solar canopies to lower peak demand charges and reduce dependence on local utility grids.
* Predictive Pipeline: Deploy an automated data pipeline that monitors regional vehicle registration velocity, triggering site upgrades and expansions 6 months before absolute capacity saturation is reached.
